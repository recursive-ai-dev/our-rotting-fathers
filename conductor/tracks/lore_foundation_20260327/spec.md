# Track Specification: Lore & World-Building Foundation

## Track Overview

**Track ID:** lore_foundation_20260327  
**Type:** Feature/Design  
**Status:** New  
**Priority:** Critical (Foundation for all subsequent development)

---

## Objectives

This track focuses on establishing the complete lore and narrative framework for "Our Rotting Father RPG" before any implementation begins. As a narrative-driven RPG set on the corpse of a rotting god, the world-building must be deep, coherent, and mechanically integrated.

### Primary Goals

1. **Faction Design:** Complete design documents for all major factions
2. **Acts Structure:** Detailed outline of all Acts with key story beats
3. **Character Lore:** Backstories, motivations, and relationships for key NPCs
4. **World Geography:** Detailed map and description of regions on the god-corpse
5. **Magic System:** Complete rules for how magic works and its costs
6. **Sanity/Psychosis Mechanics:** Narrative integration of the evolutionary trait

### 2. Acts Structure Document

#### Act 1: The Withered Hand (MVP Scope)

- Key locations to explore
- Major story beats and turning points
- Boss encounters and their significance
- Player choices and their consequences
- Act climax and resolution

- High-level outline
- New mechanics introduced

- Major revelations about the god

#### Act 3: The Bone Spires (Future)

#### Act 4: The Dreaming Mind (Finale)
- High-level outline

### 3. Character Lore Bible

#### Protagonist Options
- Background options for character creation
- Default backstory for marketing/promotion
- Connection to the world's lore

#### Key NPCs (Per Faction)
- Name, title, role
- Personal history and motivations
- Relationship to player character
- Quest involvement
- Dialogue style and personality

#### Antagonists
- Primary antagonist(s) for each Act
- Motivations and philosophy

### 4. World Geography Document

#### Regional Maps
- The Withered Hand (starting area)
  - Sub-regions and points of interest
  - Enemy types and encounters
  - Resources and crafting materials
  - Hidden secrets and easter eggs

- The Festering Heart
  - Sub-regions and points of interest
  - Environmental hazards
  - Faction strongholds

- The Bone Spires
  - Sub-regions and points of interest
  - Vertical exploration elements
  - Purified fortresses

- The Dreaming Mind
  - Surreal geography
  - Reality distortion zones
  - Prophetic vision locations

- How each region corresponds to god's anatomy
- Environmental storytelling through body parts
- Geological processes (decay, mutation, growth)

### 5. Magic System Document

#### Schools of Magic
- Necrosis Magic (decay, disease, entropy)
- Spore Magic (growth, infection, transformation)
- Bone Magic (structure, protection, animation)
- Miasma Magic (poison, madness, dreams)

#### Magic Mechanics
- Resource cost (mana, sanity, health)
- Learning requirements
- Spell progression
- Ritual vs. instant casting
- Magical items and artifacts

#### Magic and Society
- Who can use magic
- Social status of mages
- Faction attitudes toward magic
- Forbidden spells and taboos

### 6. Sanity/Psychosis Mechanics Document

#### Sanity Scale
- Range (0-100 or similar)
- Effects at different levels
- Methods to increase/decrease sanity
- Permanent vs. temporary changes

#### Psychosis Benefits
- Powers unlocked at high sanity
- Special dialogue options
- Unique quest solutions
- Combat abilities

#### Psychosis Costs
- Risks and drawbacks
- Hallucinations and unreliability
- NPC reactions
- Potential game over conditions

#### Narrative Integration
- How sanity affects the story
- Multiple endings based on sanity
- Character arc and sanity

## Acceptance Criteria

This track is complete when:

- [ ] All six deliverables are documented in markdown format
- [ ] Documents are internally consistent (no lore contradictions)
- [ ] Documents are consistent with product.md and product-guidelines.md
- [ ] Faction designs support gameplay mechanics (reputation system, quests)
- [ ] Acts structure supports MVP scope (Act 1 fully detailed, Acts 2-4 outlined)
- [ ] Magic system is balanced and integrated with combat
- [ ] Sanity mechanics are narratively meaningful (not just a stat)
- [ ] All key NPCs have complete character profiles
- [ ] World geography supports exploration and environmental storytelling
- [ ] Documents are organized in a dedicated lore directory
- [ ] User has reviewed and approved all documents

---

## Out of Scope

The following are explicitly NOT part of this track:

- Implementation of any game systems (code, assets)
- Quest scripting or dialogue tree implementation
- Level design or map creation
- Character art or visual design
- Audio design or music composition
- UI/UX mockups

These will be addressed in subsequent implementation tracks.

## Dependencies

This track has no dependencies and is a prerequisite for:
- Act 1 Implementation Track
- Character System Implementation Track
- Combat System Implementation Track
- Dialogue System Implementation Track

## Success Metrics

- **Completeness:** All six deliverables documented
- **Coherence:** No internal contradictions in lore
- **Depth:** Each faction has distinct identity, philosophy, and quest hooks
- **Integration:** Lore directly supports gameplay mechanics (sanity, magic, factions)
- **Scalability:** Framework supports future content expansion
- **User Approval:** User explicitly approves all documents

---

## Notes

- This track is design-only; no code implementation
- All documents should follow the prose style guidelines (gritty/hallucinatory)
- Lore should support multiple playthroughs with different faction allegiances
- Sanity mechanics should feel meaningful, not punitive
- Act 1 must be fully detailed for MVP; Acts 2-4 can be high-level outlines
