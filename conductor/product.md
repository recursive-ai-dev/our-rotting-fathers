# Our Rotting Father RPG - Product Definition

## Vision Statement
A narrative-driven dark fantasy turn-based RPG set on the colossal corpse of a rotting god. Players explore a civilization of human-elf creatures whose culture, religion, and very psychology have been shaped by generations of exposure to the divine decay. Their evolutionary advantage—delusion and psychosis—allows them to survive and thrive in conditions that would destroy any other species.

## World Lore & Setting

### The Rotting God
- An ancient, colossal deity whose corpse forms the entire world
- The flesh continuously decays, releasing miasma and spores that affect all life
- Different regions of the god's body create distinct biomes (The Withered Hand, The Festering Heart, The Bone Spires, etc.)

### The People - "Deludari" (Children of Delusion)
- Human-elf hybrid race evolved on the divine corpse
- **Evolutionary Trait**: Innate resistance to madness through controlled psychosis
- Their delusions manifest as protective mental filters, allowing them to function in horrific conditions
- Different factions interpret the rot differently—some worship it, some seek to cure it, some embrace it

### Factions & Belief Systems
- **The Purified**: Seek to cleanse the rot, believe salvation lies in purity
- **Rotborn Embrace**: Worship the decay as divine blessing, embrace mutation
- **The Sane Council**: Attempt to maintain rational governance despite universal madness
- **Spore Prophets**: Mystics who interpret the god's dreams through hallucinations
- Multiple schisms, holy wars, and purges throughout history

### Magic System
- No firearms—technology stalled due to reliance on magical solutions
- **Mages**: Channel the rot's energy directly, risk accelerating their own psychosis
- Different schools of magic tied to different aspects of decay (Necrosis, Spore, Bone, Miasma)
- Magic use increases exposure, creating a cost/benefit tension

## Target Audience
- **Primary**: Story-focused RPG players who value deep lore and world-building
- **Secondary**: Fans of dark fantasy (Dark Souls, Blasphemous, Salt and Sanctuary)
- **Tertiary**: Players interested in psychological horror and unreliable narrator mechanics

## Core Features

### 1. Story & Narrative
- Branching storyline exploring faction conflicts and personal sanity
- Multiple endings based on which faction prevails (or if the god awakens)
- Unreliable narrator mechanic—player's perception affected by character's psychosis level
- Rich lore delivered through:
  - Environmental storytelling (corpse anatomy as geography)
  - NPC dialogues with conflicting accounts of history
  - Item descriptions revealing faction perspectives
  - Hallucination sequences that may or may not be real

### 2. Turn-Based Combat System
- Strategic combat with sanity mechanics integrated
- **Psychosis Meter**: High levels grant power but risk losing control
- Character classes tied to faction allegiances and rot exposure
- Enemy variety:
  - Mutated creatures born from rot
  - Fanatics from opposing factions
  - Manifestations of collective hallucinations
  - Bosses representing major lore figures

### 3. Character Progression
- **Dual Progression System**:
  - Traditional: Levels, stats, equipment, abilities
  - Sanity Track: Controlled descent into madness unlocks powers but changes story
- Equipment crafted from god-flesh, bone, and purified materials
- Skill trees diverge based on faction alignment and psychosis choices
- Multiple builds encouraged through New Game+ with different faction paths

### 4. Exploration
- Interconnected world map following the god's anatomy
- Regions include:
  - The Withered Hand (starting area, Council territory)
  - The Festering Heart (Rotborn stronghold, high magic, high danger)
  - The Bone Spires (Purified fortresses, purification rituals)
  - The Dreaming Mind (late-game, prophetic visions manifest)
- Hidden secrets tied to the god's true nature and potential awakening
- Environmental hazards: Spore storms, reality distortions, madness zones

### 5. Faction System
- Reputation system with each major faction
- Choices affect which factions become allies or enemies
- Faction-specific quests, gear, and abilities
- Some areas only accessible through specific faction allegiances
- Endgame determined by which faction dominates (or player's final choice)

### 6. Presentation
- Atmospheric pixel art or hand-drawn visuals emphasizing body horror
- Color palette shifts based on character's psychosis level
- Visual hallucinations that blur reality (optional accessibility toggle)
- Dynamic soundtrack with adaptive layers (calm → frenzied based on exposure)
- Sound design: Dripping, creaking, whispering, breathing—the world feels alive/dead

## User Experience Principles

### 1. Immersive Atmosphere
- UI designed to feel like part of the world (carved bone, pulsing flesh)
- Minimalist HUD; information conveyed through character sensations
- Loading screens present as "losing consciousness" or "visions"

### 2. Accessible Depth
- Turn-based combat easy to learn, strategically deep
- Clear visual feedback on sanity effects and status
- Optional codex explaining lore terms and faction histories
- Difficulty settings affect both combat and sanity management

### 3. Meaningful Choices
- Faction allegiances lock out certain content but create unique paths
- Sanity choices affect available dialogue options and quest solutions
- No purely "good" path—every faction has valid points and dark secrets
- Personal psychosis level changes which NPCs trust you, which fear you

### 4. Respect Player Agency
- Allow players to define their character's relationship with madness
- Multiple valid approaches to quests (combat, diplomacy, deception, ritual)
- Fast travel via "Dream Gates" after discovery
- Manual and auto-save with checkpoint system

## Success Metrics
- Player engagement: Average session length >45 minutes (story immersion)
- Completion rate: >50% finish at least one faction storyline
- Replay rate: >35% start second playthrough with different faction
- Community: Active lore discussion, theory-crafting about god's true nature

## Technical Constraints
- Web-based delivery (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
- Target load time: <5 seconds initial, <2 second area transitions
- Responsive design: Desktop primary (1920x1080), tablet secondary
- Progressive Web App support for offline play
- Save data stored locally with optional cloud sync

## Content Rating Considerations
- Body horror and psychological themes (target: T for Teen / PEGI 16)
- Violence: Fantasy combat, no gore for gore's sake
- Themes: Mental illness portrayed respectfully as evolutionary trait
- Optional content warnings for intense sequences
