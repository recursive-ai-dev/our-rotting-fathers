# The Purified

## Overview

**"The flesh is false. The bone is truth. Death is escape."**

The Purified are not merely those who reject the rot—they are those who have declared **war on existence itself**. They believe that to be born on the corpse is to be born into corruption, and the only true purity is **the cessation of being**. They do not seek to cleanse the rot. They seek to cleanse **life**.

They are the only faction that has looked upon the rotting god and concluded: *"We should not be here. We should not be anywhere. Let us unmake ourselves."*

---

## Philosophy and Core Beliefs

### The Doctrine of Unmaking

The Purified hold a truth so absolute it burns: **existence is the disease**. The rot is merely a symptom. The god is dead, yes—but the greater tragedy is that **anything lives on its corpse**.

Their philosophy rests on three pillars:

1. **The Flesh is False** - All life on the god is parasitic. Every beating heart is a theft from the silence. Every breath is an insult to the peace of the dead god.
2. **The Bone is Truth** - Only bone endures. Only bone is honest. Flesh decays, lies, breeds. Bone remains. To become bone is to become true.
3. **Death is Deliverance** - The only escape from the corpse is to join it in death. Not through suicide (that is weakness) but through **systematic unmaking**—the deliberate stripping away of everything that makes you alive, until only truth remains.

### The Creation Myth (Revised)

The Purified tell the only story that matters:

> *"The god did not die. The god was murdered.*
>
> *By whom? By what? We do not know. But we know this: something killed perfection. And in killing it, created imperfection. Us.*
>
> *We are the wound that will not close. We are the infection spreading across the corpse. We are the mistake that learned to walk and talk and breed.*
>
> *There is no salvation. There is only correction. We must unmake ourselves. We must return the god to peace. We must become the silence we should have been."*

This myth does not offer hope. It offers **purpose**: the purpose of ending.

### Moral Framework

**Virtues:**
- **Stillness** - The closer to death, the closer to truth. Movement is lies.
- **Silence** - Speech is the noise of living flesh. Quiet is the language of bone.
- **Emptiness** - Remove all that makes you "you." Memories, desires, fears. These are the rot of self.
- **Finality** - Every act should move you toward ending. Incremental unmaking is still unmaking.

**Sins:**
- **Creation** - To make something new is to add to the infection. Art, children, buildings—all are crimes.
- **Joy** - Pleasure is the lie the flesh tells to perpetuate itself.
- **Hope** - The cruelest delusion. There is no future. There is only the long descent into silence.
- **Selfhood** - To have a "personality," "dreams," "identity" is to be deeply corrupted.

**Taboos:**
- **Procreation** - The ultimate sin. To birth new life is to sentence an innocent to the disease of existence. (Punishable by immediate unmaking)
- **Healing** - To heal a wound is to prolong suffering. To cure disease is to deny the truth of flesh.
- **Laughter** - The sound of a mind still believing in lies.
- **Names** - Each person has one name, given at birth. To use it is to acknowledge the fiction of identity. The Purified refer to each other by number, or not at all.

## Hierarchy and Leadership

### Ranks and Titles (The Stages of Unmaking)

The Purified are organized by **how much of themselves they have removed**. Each rank represents a stage of systematic unmaking.

| Rank | Title | What Has Been Removed |
|------|-------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Flesh-Bound** | Nothing yet. Initiates still believe they are "people." |
| 2 | **Memory-Stripped** | Memories removed via ritual lobotomy. No past, only function. |
| 3 | **Desire-Culled** | All wants, dreams, preferences excised. No future, only duty. |
| 4 | **Fear-Burned** | Fear centers cauterized. No self-preservation, only purpose. |
| 5 | **Name-Erased** | Personal identity dissolved. No self, only service. |
| 6 | **Hollow** | Everything removed except motor function and obedience. |
| 7 | **The First Silence** | Supreme leader. Has undergone complete unmaking while remaining animate. A walking corpse that speaks the god's silence. |

**Note:** The progression is not metaphorical. Each rank involves **surgical, magical, or ritual removal** of specific aspects of humanity. By rank 5, members are barely recognizable as alive.

### Leadership Council: The Circle of Ash (The Seven Hollows)

The Purified are ruled by **The Circle of Ash**—seven Hollows who have removed everything except the minimum neural function required to command. They do not "meet." They do not "discuss." They exist in a shared silence, and their decisions emerge like corpses from graves.

**The Seven Hollows:**

- **The First Silence** - Supreme leader. Has not spoken in 47 years. Communicates through gestures that no one remembers learning, yet all understand.
- **The Memory of Pain** - Oversees unmaking rituals. Remembers only the sensation of pain, nothing else. Uses this to guide surgical precision.
- **The Hand That Cuts** - Military commander. Has removed all brain hemispheres except motor control and tactical processing. Does not sleep. Does not blink unless commanded.
- **The Empty Womb** - Chief theologian. Was once pregnant; chose to excise the fetus as "the ultimate correction." Now preaches the sin of creation with the authority of one who has unmade a life.
- **The Tongue of Silence** - Intelligence master. Had their tongue removed; now speaks directly into minds through necromantic magic. Hears all thoughts within Purified territory.
- **The Bone Reader** - Chief architect. Sees only skeletal structures. Designs buildings by flaying away flesh-vision and drawing what remains.
- **The Last Breath** - Master of unmaking rites. Has died 12 times; each time, was resurrected with less of a soul. Now is mostly hollow, filled with ash and divine silence.

### Succession and Promotion

**Promotion** is not earned. It is **excised**.

When a member is deemed ready for the next stage of unmaking, they are taken to the **Operating Theaters of the Bone Spires**. There, the Hollows perform the appropriate removal:

- **Flesh-Bound → Memory-Stripped:** Ritual lobotomy. The subject is awake (anesthesia is "coddling the flesh"). Memories are scooped out with bone-spoons. The subject forgets their name, their family, their childhood. They remember only their function.
- **Memory-Stripped → Desire-Culled:** Chemical and magical castration of the dream-centers. The subject loses all capacity for hope, ambition, preference. They no longer want anything. They no longer imagine tomorrow.
- **Desire-Culled → Fear-Burned:** The amygdala is cauterized with white-hot bone-needles. The subject loses all fear—including fear of death. They become perfectly obedient, perfectly still, perfectly empty.
- **Fear-Burned → Name-Erased:** The subject's name is carved from their flesh (literally—cut from their skin, burned from their bones). They are given a number. They respond to it. They do not remember having been anything else.
- **Name-Erased → Hollow:** The final removal. Everything that could be called "self" is excised. What remains is a breathing machine of bone and ash, animated by hatred of life itself.

**Succession** to the Circle of Ash:
- When a Hollow dies (or achieves true death), the remaining Hollows select a replacement from the Name-Erased.
- The candidate is subjected to **Total Unmaking**—a ritual so extreme that 97% die during the process.
- The 3% who survive emerge as something no longer human. They join the Circle. They never speak again.

## Territory and Architecture

### The Bone Spires (The Ossuary of Final Silence)

The Purified control **the Bone Spires**—a region where the god's bones protrude through the rotting flesh like ribs through skin. This is not a "safe" region. It is a **dead** region. Nothing grows here. Nothing breeds. The wind howls through hollow bones and sounds like screaming.

**Geography:**
- **The Seven Spires** - Massive bone-formations, each hollowed out and converted into a temple of unmaking
- **The Ash Wastes** - Flat expanse of powdered bone and ash, swept by winds that carry the whispers of the unmade
- **The Operating Theaters** - Where unmaking rituals are performed. The ground is stained with memories (literally—psychic residue pools in the cracks)
- **The Cathedral of Final Silence** - Central temple, built inside the largest spire. Contains the **Tomb of the First Silence**, where the supreme leader sits in eternal catatonia

### Architectural Principles

Purified architecture is not designed to shelter the living. It is designed to **accelerate unmaking**.

1. **Angular** - Curves remind the flesh of its own softness. Angles cut. Angles wound. Angles remind the body that it is temporary.
2. **Sterile** - No decoration. No art. No color beyond white, gray, and the pale blue of bruised flesh. To decorate is to lie.
3. **Hostile** - Floors are uneven (to remind the body of its fragility). Air is filtered to remove all spores, all scent, all taste. Light is harsh, shadowless, revealing every imperfection.

**Materials:**
- **Bone-Steel** - Bone treated until it is harder than metal, cold as death
- **Ash-Crete** - Concrete mixed with the powdered remains of the unmade (every building contains the dead)
- **Memory-Glass** - Glass infused with lobotomized memories; looking through it shows you visions of lives you no longer have
- **Flesh-Repellent** - Coatings that cause organic matter to slide off, preventing any suggestion of "growth" or "life"

### Key Locations

#### The Cathedral of Final Silence (formerly "Final Cleansing")
- **Purpose**: Not a temple of worship—a temple of **ending**. Where the final unmaking rites are performed.
- **Features**: 
  - The **Tomb of the First Silence** - A sealed chamber where the supreme leader sits catatonic, surrounded by the preserved bones of all previous First Silences. Pilgrims come to hear the silence speak to them (it does not; they project their own longing for nothingness).
  - The **Hall of Excised Memories** - Walls lined with glass jars containing the scooped-out memories of the Memory-Stripped. Each jar is labeled with a number. None are ever opened.
  - The **Chamber of White Fire** - Where the Fear-Burned undergo amygdala cauterization. The fire is fueled by the fat of the unmade.

#### The Spire of the First Silence
- **Purpose**: Residence of the supreme leader (who does not reside—they **exist**, minimally).
- **Features**:
  - The **Observatory of Emptiness** - Telescopes pointed not at stars, but at the void between stars. Astronomers study nothingness and report their findings to the Hollows (the findings are always the same: there is nothing, and this is good).
  - The **War Room of Final Tactics** - Where military strategy is decided by the Hand That Cuts. The room contains no maps, only lists of targets to be unmade.
  - The **Sanctum of Unbeing** - Private chambers of the First Silence. No one has entered in 47 years. Sounds emerge sometimes. They do not sound human.

#### The Operating Theaters
- **Purpose**: Where unmaking rituals are performed. This is not a hospital. Hospitals heal. The Theaters **correct**.
- **Features**:
  - **Seven Theaters** - Each dedicated to a stage of unmaking (Memory, Desire, Fear, Name, Self, Soul, Silence)
  - **The Offering Tables** - Where the excised parts are placed before incineration (memories scream when burned; desires weep; fears laugh)
  - **Ash Collection** - The remains are not discarded. They are mixed into ash-crete and used to build new Theaters. The dead build the death-culture.

#### The Fortress of the Hollow
- **Purpose**: Military barracks. Home of the Hand That Cuts and the armies of the unmade.
- **Features**:
  - **The Proving Grounds** - Where Flesh-Bound initiates are tested. They are placed in rooms with their own memories (manifested through magic) and must cut them away to prove readiness.
  - **The Armory** - Weapons are not stored here; they are **mounted**, like trophies. Each weapon is labeled with the number of lives it has unmade.
  - **The Hospital of White Fire** - A "hospital" that does not heal. It triages. Those who can be unmade further are sent to the Theaters. Those who cannot are sent to the Ash Wastes to die.

## Relationships with Other Factions

### The Purified vs. Rotborn Embrace

**Status: Existential War**

The Rotborn are not enemies. They are **an affront to the concept of existence itself**. If the Purified believe life is a disease, the Rotborn believe the disease is divine. This is not a conflict that can be resolved. One side must cease to be.

**The Horror:** The Purified do not hate the Rotborn. Hate would require caring. They view the Rotborn as a surgeon views a tumor—with the detached certainty that it must be cut away.

**Military Posture:**
- The **Hand That Cuts** leads eternal campaigns along the border with the Festering Heart
- Rotborn prisoners are not killed. They are **unmade**—slowly, publicly, as a lesson
- The Purified do not take territory. They leave behind only ash and silence. A conquered Rotborn settlement is not occupied; it is **erased**

### The Purified vs. The Delusion Architects (formerly "Sane Council")

**Status: Uneasy Non-Aggression**

The Delusion Architects are fools who have looked into the abyss and decided to **decorate it**. They accept that psychosis is inevitable, so they curate it—engineering collective delusions to keep the masses functional. To the Purified, this is the worst sin of all: not embracing rot (like the Rotborn) but **lying about it**.

**The Horror:** The Purified know what the Delusion Architects do not admit: the delusions do not work. They never worked. The god is still dead. The rot still spreads. The only difference is that the Architects die confused, while the Purified die **enlightened**.

**Diplomatic Stance:**
- Trade is permitted (the Architects sell "sanctioned hallucinations"—useless to the Purified, but the ash-crete they build with is sturdy)
- The Purified occasionally "liberate" Architects citizens and offer them unmaking. Most refuse. The Purified do not try again.
- When the Architects' delusions fail (and they do, regularly), the Purified are there to collect the broken survivors and offer them the only truth that matters: silence

### The Purified vs. The God's Nervous System (formerly "Spore Prophets")

**Status: Theological Extermination**

The God's Nervous System are not a faction. They are a **symptom**. They implant spores into their brainstems and become living synapses for the god's dying neural firings. They do not "prophesize"—they experience the god's death-throes as reality.

To the Purified, this is the ultimate obscenity: **the god should be silent, and they make it speak**.

**The Horror:** The Purified are right. The Prophets are wrong. But the Prophets are also **dangerous**, because sometimes, when the god twitches in its sleep, the Prophets twitch with it. And when enough Prophets twitch at once, cities vanish.

**Military Posture:**
- The Purified do not negotiate with symptoms. They excise them.
- Prophet settlements are not conquered; they are **lobotomized**—every spore-infected brain is drilled, every spore flushed with boiling ash-water
- The Purified collect the excised spores and burn them in the Chamber of White Fire. The fire screams. They do not extinguish it. They let it burn as penance

## Key NPCs

### The First Silence (formerly "First Pure Malachi the Unburned")

**Title:** Supreme Leader of the Purified  
**Age:** Unknown (stopped counting 47 years ago)  
**Appearance:** A skeleton wrapped in desiccated skin, stretched tight like parchment. No hair. No eyes (removed during unmaking—sight is a lie). Mouth sewn shut with silver wire (speech is a lie). Stands perfectly motionless for days. When it moves, it moves with the jerky precision of a corpse animated by necromancy. Wears robes woven from the hair of the Memory-Stripped.

**History:**
The First Silence was once called Malachi. That name has been carved from every record, burned from every bone that remembered it. What remains is a creature that sits on a throne of calcified nervous system in the Tomb of the First Silence, surrounded by the bones of all previous First Silences.

Forty-seven years ago, Malachi underwent the **Total Unmaking**—a ritual so extreme that the subject's soul is flayed away layer by layer until only the minimum neural function remains. Malachi survived. What emerged does not eat (food is a lie of the flesh). Does not sleep (dreams are lies of the mind). Does not speak (language is a lie of culture).

It sits. It waits. It listens to the silence of the dead god. And sometimes, when the Hollows cannot decide, it gestures—with a finger that has not moved in decades—and they understand.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To achieve the Final Silence—the moment when the last living thing on the god's corpse stops breathing
- **Secondary:** To hear the god speak one word before it dies (it has been listening for 47 years; it has heard nothing)
- **Hidden:** Sometimes, in the deepest watches of the night, the First Silence twitches. The Hollows pretend not to notice.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Purified: The First Silence does not acknowledge the player. Ever. The player sits in the Tomb and listens to silence. Some players go mad. Some go enlightened.
- If player opposes the Purified: The First Silence does not order your death. It orders your **unmaking**. There is a difference. Death is mercy. Unmaking is correction.
- If player is neutral: The First Silence dreams of you. You do not know this. But sometimes, when you close your eyes, you feel a presence. That is it. Practicing.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The Sound of Silence" - Player must sit in the Tomb for 7 days without speaking, eating, or sleeping. Most fail. Those who succeed are... changed.
- **Major Chain:** "The Seven Unmakings" - Player witnesses each stage of unmaking performed on a willing (unwilling? both?) subject. By the seventh, the player must decide: is this mercy or murder?
- **Endgame:** "The Last Word" - The First Silence finally hears the god speak. It is one word. The player must choose: hear it (and go mad) or silence the First Silence forever (and never know)

**Dialogue:**
The First Silence does not speak. It gestures. The Hollows interpret. Their interpretations are never questioned.

**Sample Gestures:**
- *One finger raised* - "Proceed."
- *Two fingers, touching* - "Wait."
- *Hand over heart* - "You are already dead. You simply have not stopped moving yet."
- *Both hands, palms down* - "Enough. The silence has heard enough."

### The Hand That Cuts (formerly "Incarnate Kael the Scarred")

**Title:** Military Commander of the Purified  
**Age:** Irrelevant (time is a lie of the living)  
**Appearance:** A torso mounted on a chassis of bone-steel. Legs amputated at the hip (legs were "distractions"). Arms replaced with articulated bone-blades (the left for cutting flesh, the right for cutting memory). Head shaved, skull carved with tactical sigils that glow when processing targets. No eyes—sees through sonar-magic that detects movement and heat. Does not breathe through lungs (breathing is weakness); breathes through a hole drilled directly into the trachea, filtered through ash.

**History:**
The Hand That Cuts was once Kael. During the Battle of the Festering Heart, Kael's body was 73% destroyed by Rotborn bio-artillery. The Hollows offered a choice: die, or become weapon. Kael chose weapon.

Over five years, every part of Kael that was not essential to killing was removed. Lungs (replaced with ash-filtration). Stomach (nutrition is delivered directly to the bloodstream via enema). Heart (replaced with a pump of bone and muscle, never resting). Brain (hemispheres responsible for empathy, art, and memory were excised; tactical processing was enhanced).

What remains is a creature that does not sleep, does not tire, does not question. It processes targets. It eliminates them. It processes the next target. It has not had a thought that was not about killing in 12 years.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To eliminate all threats to the Purified (currently: Rotborn, Prophets, existence)
- **Secondary:** To find a target it cannot kill (it has not found one; it is frustrated)
- **Hidden:** Sometimes, in the silence between heartbeats, it hears a whisper: *"Why?"* It does not know what the whisper means. It kills faster.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Purified: The Hand assigns the player to elimination squads. It watches. If the player shows weakness, it recommends unmaking. If the player shows strength, it recommends... further enhancement.
- If player opposes the Purified: The Hand processes you as a target. It does not hate you. It does not feel anything. You are a coordinate. You will be eliminated.
- If player is neutral: The Hand ignores you. You are not a threat. You are not a weapon. You are... furniture.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "Prove Your Weapon" - Player must spar with the Hand (unarmed). Surviving for 30 seconds is "passing." (No one has passed.)
- **Major Chain:** "The Elimination Protocols" - Player accompanies the Hand on target eliminations. Each target is more sympathetic than the last. By the end, the player must decide: is the Hand a monster, or is it honest?
- **Endgame:** "The Unkillable Target" - The Hand encounters a target it cannot kill (a child? a memory? itself?). It malfunctions. The player must choose: repair it, or put it down.

**Dialogue Style:**
Tactical. Fragmented. No pronouns except "target" and "weapon." Voice is synthesized through a bone-vocoder. Sounds like grinding teeth.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"Target: identified. Threat: negligible. Elimination: scheduled. Duration: 4.3 minutes. Begin."*
>
> *"Weapon: you. Enhancement: required. Left arm: inefficient. Removal: recommended. Consent: irrelevant."*
>
> *"Question: why do you fight? Answer: you do not. Fighting is what happens to you. You are the place where violence occurs. Accept this."*

### The Empty Womb (formerly "Incarnate Vesper the Clear-Eyed")

**Title:** Chief Theologian of the Purified  
**Age:** 44 (counts only the years since the excision)  
**Appearance:** Female-form, emaciated. Abdomen is a hollow cavity—ribs curved inward, creating a cage where organs should be. Inside the cage burns a white fire (the "fire of truth"). When she speaks, the fire flickers. No reproductive organs (removed during the ritual of Final Correction). Skin is translucent—you can see the bones, the veins, the absence. Wears robes of white linen, open at the front (nothing to hide).

**History:**
The Empty Womb was once Vesper, a Spore Prophet of great power. She could hear the god's dreams more clearly than any Prophet in her generation. She could feel the neural firings, the death-throes, the last synapses sparking out one by one.

And then she became pregnant.

For nine months, she listened to two things: the dying god, and the growing life inside her. And she realized: they are the same. Both are noise. Both are lies. Both must end.

On the ninth month, she performed the ritual of Final Correction on herself. With bone-knives and ash-water, she excised the fetus. She burned it in the Chamber of White Fire. And as it burned, she heard something she had never heard before: **silence**.

She defected to the Purified that day. She preaches the sin of creation with the authority of one who has unmade a life—and found salvation in the ending.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To convince all parents to unmake their children (mercy, not cruelty)
- **Secondary:** To find a theology that does not require gods (the god is dead; theology should be too)
- **Hidden:** Sometimes, she hears crying. Not from the fire. From inside her own hollow chest. She knows it is not real. She listens anyway.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Purified: The Empty Womb tests the player's commitment. She asks: "What have you unmade?" If the player has children, she asks: "Why are they still alive?"
- If player opposes the Purified: She does not fight. She preaches. She tells the player about the silence after the burning. She watches the player's face. Some faces flicker.
- If player has children: She sends letters. They contain ash. And a single question: *"Do you love them enough to end them?"*

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The Sermon of the Hollow" - Player attends a sermon. The Empty Womb opens her chest-cage and shows the fire. Some congregants weep. Some vomit. Some... understand.
- **Major Chain:** "The Mercy of Ending" - Player is sent to "counsel" parents who have refused to unmake their children. Some can be convinced. Some cannot. The player must decide: help the Empty Womb, or help the parents hide.
- **Endgame:** "The Last Cry" - The Empty Womb's hollow chest begins to... grow something. Not a child. Something worse. She asks the player to excise it. Or to let it grow. Or to burn her entire body and end the question forever.

**Dialogue Style:**
Soft. Maternal. Like a mother singing a lullaby about how good it will be to stop breathing. When she becomes passionate, the fire in her chest flares, and her voice becomes multiple voices (the voices of all the unmade children she has "saved").

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"You think I am cruel. I understand. I was a mother once. For nine months, I listened to a heart that was not mine. It beat. And beat. And I loved it.*
>
> *And then I understood: to love it is to end it. To bring it into this world is to sentence it to rot. To rot is to suffer. To suffer is to... continue.*
>
> *I gave it the only gift a mother can give: silence.*
>
> *Do you love your children? Then you know what you must do."*

**Title:** Flesh-Bound Initiate (lowest rank; has not yet been unmade)  
**Age:** 28 (counts, because he has not been told to stop)  
**Appearance:** Average. Unremarkable. This is the point. The Purified do not want individuals; they want raw material. Short brown hair (will be shaved during Memory-Stripping). Left cheek discolored from rot exposure (will be cut away during Desire-Culling). Eyes that have not yet learned to look away. Wears the gray tunic of initiates—soon to be replaced by the white robes of the Memory-Stripped.

**History:**
Subject 734 was once Jorin. He was a spore-harvester for the Delusion Architects, collecting spores for "medicinal hallucinations." He believed he was helping people. He was wrong.

The rot-marks appeared on his arm. The Architects exiled him ("for quarantine"). The Purified found him. They offered him a choice: unmaking, or death. He chose unmaking.

He has been Flesh-Bound for three months. He still remembers his name. He still dreams. He still wakes up crying. The Hollows have noticed. They are... disappointed.

He knows what comes next. The Memory-Stripping. The lobotomy. The end of Jorin, and the beginning of 734. He is not ready. He will never be ready. But he will undergo it anyway. Because the alternative is worse: to remain Jorin, forever.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To survive the Memory-Stripping without losing... something. He does not know what. But something matters. Something is worth keeping.
- **Secondary:** To understand why the Purified are so certain. Certainty this absolute must be based on... something. He hopes.
- **Hidden:** He has started to hear the silence. It calls to him. Some days, he answers.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Purified: 734 is the player's guide. He shows the player the ropes. He does not mention that he cries at night. He does not mention that he is afraid. Fear is a sin.
- If player opposes the Purified: 734 is sent to unmake the player. He hesitates. The player notices. This hesitation will cost 734 his memories. Or his life.
- If player is neutral: 734 asks questions. "Do you still remember your mother's face? Does it matter? Will it matter after the spoon scoops?" The player does not have answers. 734 files this away.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The Last Memory" - 734 asks the player to help him hide one memory before the lobotomy. A small memory. A worthless memory. The memory of a flower, maybe. The player helps. Or does not.
- **Major Chain:** "The Counting" - 734 starts counting everything. The number of steps to the Theater. The number of breaths per minute. The number of heartbeats until the procedure. He says: "If I count enough, I will not forget." The player knows he is wrong. The player helps anyway.
- **Endgame:** "Subject 734" - The procedure is complete. 734 is gone. What remains is a hollow thing that responds to its number. It does not remember the player. It does not remember the flower. The player must choose: is this mercy? Or should the player have killed 734 instead?

**Dialogue Style:**
Human. Afraid. Trying to be brave. Uses metaphors. Remembers things. Talks about the future (a sin). When he speaks of the procedure, his voice becomes small.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"I know what they say. 'Memory is a lie. The past is a wound that will not close.' I know. I do. But... my mother's face. She had a scar. Here. On her chin. She got it saving me from... something. I don't remember what.*
>
> *They're going to take that. The scar. Her face. Me.*
>
> *Is that... is that okay? Does it have to be?*
>
> *You've been where I'm going. You've... unmade. Did you keep anything? Or is it all... gone?"*

## Quest Hooks and Story Arcs

### Introduction Quest Line: "The Path to Unmaking"

**Quest 1: "The First Lie"**
- **Trigger:** Player enters Purified territory
- **Objective:** Submit to evaluation. The Hollows assess how much "self" the player has.
- **Choice:** Admit you want to be unmade (lie) or admit you're afraid (truth). Both are accepted; the lie is noted.
- **Reward:** Rank of Flesh-Bound, access to Purified territory (and Operating Theaters)

**Quest 2: "The Memory Room"**
- **Trigger:** Completion of "The First Lie"
- **Objective:** Player is placed in a room with their own memories (manifested through magic). They must cut them away, one by one.
- **Encounter:** Psychological horror. Each memory fights back. The memory of a lover begs. The memory of a child cries. The memory of a parent screams.
- **Reward:** Rank progression. First taste of silence.

**Quest 3: "The Spoon"**
- **Trigger:** Completion of "The Memory Room"
- **Objective:** Player witnesses a Memory-Stripping. The subject is awake. The Hollow uses a bone-spoon. The player must hold the subject down.
- **Choice:** Hold them gently (mercy) or firmly (efficiency). The subject screams either way.
- **Reward:** Rank of... witness. Not yet Memory-Stripped. Not yet one of them. But closer.

### Major Faction Quest Chain: "The Seven Unmakings"

**Overview:** Seven trials. Seven removals. Seven steps toward silence. Each trial is named for what is taken.

**Unmaking 1: "The Memory"**
- **Objective:** Undergo voluntary Memory-Stripping. Keep one memory (hidden from the Hollows).
- **Gameplay:** Skill challenge: hide the memory during the procedure. If discovered, the Hollows take it anyway—and take something else as penalty.
- **Reward:** Rank of Memory-Stripped. Access to... emptiness. The player forgets why they started.

**Unmaking 2: "The Desire"**
- **Objective:** Identify all wants, dreams, preferences. Write them down. Burn the list.
- **Gameplay:** Introspection quest. Player must catalog their desires. Some are easy (food, comfort). Some are hard (love, purpose, meaning).
- **Reward:** Rank of Desire-Culled. Player no longer wants anything. Quests become... mechanical.

**Unmaking 3: "The Fear"**
- **Objective:** Submit to amygdala cauterization. Feel no fear. Ever.
- **Gameplay:** Boss fight against the player's greatest fear (manifested). After defeating it, the procedure. After the procedure, the player walks into a room of horrors and feels... nothing.
- **Reward:** Rank of Fear-Burned. Immunity to fear effects. Loss of self-preservation.

**Unmaking 4: "The Name"**
- **Objective:** Carve your name from your flesh. Burn it from your bones. Receive a number.
- **Gameplay:** Ritual quest. Player chooses which body parts to carve (each choice affects stats). The Hollows assign a number. The player responds to it.
- **Reward:** Rank of Name-Erased. NPCs no longer see the player as a person. Some quests become unavailable. Some... open.

**Unmaking 5: "The Self"**
- **Objective:** Everything that is "you" is excised. Personality. History. Preferences. Relationships. What remains?
- **Gameplay:** The player becomes an NPC for one quest. They watch their own body move, act, kill. They do not control it. They are... passenger.
- **Reward:** Rank of Hollow. Player is now an automaton. Quests are given, not chosen.

**Unmaking 6: "The Soul"**
- **Objective:** The soul is removed. Not metaphorically. Literally. It screams when pulled.
- **Gameplay:** Astral quest. Player follows their own soul as it is dragged from their body. They can try to catch it. They will fail.
- **Reward:** Rank of... almost-Silence. Player is now a weapon. No longer a character. A tool.

**Unmaking 7: "The Silence"**
- **Objective:** Final unmaking. Become the First Silence. Or do not.
- **Choice:** Complete the unmaking (become an NPC, end the player's story) or... stop. Reject the path. Fight the Hollows. Kill the First Silence.
- **Reward:** Multiple endings. See "Endgame Faction Resolution."

### Endgame Faction Resolution

**Scenario 1: The Final Silence (Complete Unmaking)**
- **Condition:** Player completes all seven unmakings
- **Outcome:** The player becomes the new First Silence. The old one dies (finally). The player sits. The player waits. The player listens. The game ends. The player wins by ceasing to play.
- **Player Reward:** "Achievement Unlocked: You Have Won By Losing." New Game+ option: start as a new Flesh-Bound, guided by your own silent self.

**Scenario 2: The Rejection (Violent Apostasy)**
- **Condition:** Player rejects the final unmaking, kills the Hollows
- **Outcome:** The Purified faction collapses. The Bone Spires fall into chaos. Some Hollows continue unmaking. Some Flesh-Bound flee. The player is... free. But haunted by the silence they almost joined.
- **Player Reward:** "Apostate" title. Access to Purified abilities (without the cost). New quests: hunt down the fleeing Hollows. Or save them.

**Scenario 3: The Compromise (The Middle Silence)**
- **Condition:** Player negotiates a third path—unmaking without death
- **Outcome:** The Purified learn to remove only the rot, not the self. They become... healers. Horrific healers, but healers. The Bone Spires become a hospital for the rot-touched. The First Silence dies, smiling (for the first time in 47 years).
- **Player Reward:** "Reformer" title. New faction: The Silent Healers. Abilities that remove rot without removing self.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**
- **White** (#FFFFFF) - Purity, cleanliness, sterility
- **Pale Blue** (#E8F4F8) - Cold, clinical, detached
- **Silver** (#C0C0C0) - Bone-steel, weapons, armor
- **Ash Gray** (#B2BEB5) - Sacred ash, purification residue

**Accent Colors:**
- **Flame Orange** (#FF8C42) - Sacred fires, used sparingly
- **Blood Red** (#8B0000) - Only used for enemies, rot, corruption

**Usage Guidelines:**
- White dominates architecture, clothing, banners
- Blue and silver for trim, details, rank insignia
- Orange only for sacred flames and ritual contexts
- Red never used in Purified contexts (reserved for enemies)

### Clothing and Armor Styles

**Civilian Clothing:**
- Simple, functional garments in white or pale blue
- No ornamentation beyond sacred geometry embroidery
- Materials: Purified linen, treated cotton, bone-fiber blends
- Coverage: Full-body coverage to minimize rot exposure

**Military Armor:**
- **Bone-Steel Plate:** Polished to mirror finish, angular design
- **Joint Protection:** Sealed joints to prevent rot particle ingress
- **Helmet:** Full-face coverage with filtered air intake
- **Insignia:** Rank marked by colored shoulder pauldrons (white = recruit, blue = cleansed, silver = purifier)

**Religious Vestments:**
- **Flamekeepers:** White robes with orange trim, flame-pattern embroidery
- **Incarnates:** White robes with silver thread, sacred geometry patterns
- **First Pure:** Pure white robes, no ornamentation (purity needs no decoration)

### Symbols and Iconography

**Primary Symbol: The White Flame**
- Stylized flame in white and silver
- Represents purification through fire
- Used on banners, armor, architecture

**Secondary Symbols:**
- **The Cleansed Bone** - Stylized bone segment, represents purity of flesh
- **The Seven Points** - Seven-pointed star, represents the seven sacred fires
- **The Ascending Spiral** - Outward spiral, represents escape from the corpse

**Sacred Geometry:**
- Straight lines, sharp angles, perfect circles
- No organic curves or irregular shapes
- Patterns based on mathematical ratios (golden mean, sacred geometry)

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Symbols always rendered in white, silver, or pale blue
- Never rotated or distorted (symmetry = purity)
- Size proportional to importance (larger = more sacred)

## Faction Mechanics (Gameplay Integration)

### Reputation System

**Reputation Scale:** -100 to +100

| Reputation | Title | Effects |
|------------|-------|---------|
| -100 to -76 | **Abomination** | Attacked on sight, no interaction |
| -75 to -51 | **Corrupt** | Hostile, can be bribed to leave |
| -50 to -26 | **Suspect** | Restricted access, higher prices |
| -25 to +25 | **Neutral** | Standard access, standard prices |
| +26 to +50 | **Cleansed** | Friendly, discounts, basic quests |
| +51 to +75 | **Purified** | Trusted, full access, faction quests |
| +76 to +100 | **Incarnate** | Honorary member, unique abilities |

**Reputation Changes:**
- **+5:** Complete faction quest
- **+10:** Complete major faction quest
- **+25:** Complete faction quest chain
- **-5:** Fail faction quest
- **-10:** Attack faction member (non-lethal)
- **-50:** Kill faction member
- **+3:** Donate purified materials
- **+5:** Successfully undergo purification ritual

### Faction Abilities

**Unlocked at Reputation +26 (Cleansed):**
- **Purification Ritual** - Remove rot effects from self or allies
- **Rot Detection** - Sense rot contamination in areas and items
- **Cleanse Wound** - Heal HP and reduce rot exposure

**Unlocked at Reputation +51 (Purified):**
- **White Fire** - Deal bonus damage to rot-touched enemies
- **Bone-Steel Skin** - Temporary resistance to rot damage
- **Clarity of Mind** - Immunity to sanity attacks for short duration

**Unlocked at Reputation +76 (Incarnate):**
- **Ascendant Form** - Temporary transformation, enhanced abilities
- **Final Purification** - Instant-kill vs. heavily corrupted enemies
- **Eternal Flame** - Summon sacred fire for area damage over time

### Faction Vendors

**The Purified Armory:**
- Sells purified weapons and armor
- Accepts only purified currency (ash-coins)
- Discounts based on reputation

**The Flamekeeper's Supplies:**
- Sells purification rituals, cleansing items, anti-rot consumables
- Run by Flamekeepers
- Prices decrease with reputation

**The Hospital of White Fire:**
- Medical services, rot treatment, sanity restoration
- Most effective healing in the game (but expensive)
- Free for high-reputation players

## Notes for Implementation

- **Tone:** Clinical, cold, but not evil. The Purified believe they are right—not cruel.
- **Visuals:** Emphasize cleanliness, sterility, angular design. Contrast with organic rot aesthetics.
- **Music:** Minimalist, cold, choral or ambient. No warm instruments.
- **Quests:** Always offer choice between purification (difficult, risky) and refusal (consequences).
- **Endgame:** The Purified should have a viable ending—not a "wrong" ending, but a costly one.
