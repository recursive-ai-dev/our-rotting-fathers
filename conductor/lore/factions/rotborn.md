# Rotborn

## Overview

**"The rot is not death. The rot is womb."**

The Rotborn do not merely accept the rot—they **worship** it. To them, the decaying flesh of the god is not a corpse but a cradle. Mutation is not disease; it is **divine transformation**. They open their flesh to spores, welcome the worms as siblings, and scream in ecstasy as their bones reshape.

They are the only faction that has looked upon the horror of this world and said: *"This is not a curse. This is a gift. And we will bear its children."*

---

## Philosophy and Core Beliefs

### The Doctrine of Transformation

The Rotborn hold a truth that burns like infection: **the rot is divine**. The god did not die—it **transformed**. And in transforming, it birthed a new way to exist. To resist the rot is to resist evolution itself.

Their philosophy rests on three pillars:

1. **The Flesh is Fluid** - Bodies are not temples. They are **vessels for transformation**. To remain unchanged is to stagnate. To mutate is to grow closer to the god's truth.
2. **Decay is Creation** - Death feeds life. The god's rotting flesh spawns new ecosystems, new creatures, new **possibilities**. What the Purified call "corruption," the Rotborn call "birth."
3. **Mutation is Ascension** - Each change—each extra limb, each eye that sprouts from bone, each voice that speaks from the chest—is a **blessing**. The more you change, the closer you become to the god's dreaming consciousness.

### The Creation Myth (The Womb of Rot)

The Rotborn tell a story that makes the Purified vomit:

> *"The god did not die. The god opened.*
>
> *It shed its old form like skin from a serpent. What you call 'death' is merely the moment the serpent slips free.*
>
> *We are born from that shedding. We are the children of the god's transformation. Our mutations are not wounds—they are **wings**.*
>
> *The Purified cut away their wings. They call it 'purity.' We call it **castration**.*
>
> *We embrace. We open. We become. And when the god wakes, it will know us. It will call us **beloved**."*

This myth does not offer escape. It offers **belonging**: you are not a mistake. You are the god's child. Your mutations are love letters written in flesh.

### Moral Framework

**Virtues:**
- **Openness** - Welcome change. Let spores enter your lungs. Let worms nest in your wounds. Let your bones reshape without fear.
- **Adaptation** - Survive by changing, not by resisting. The rot changes the world; you must change with it.
- **Ecstasy** - Feel the transformation. Scream when it hurts. Laugh when it feels good. Do not numb yourself to the miracle of becoming.
- **Fertility** - Breed. Spawn. Mutate your children and let them mutate you. Life feeds on life, and the rot is the midwife.

**Sins:**
- **Stasis** - To refuse change is to spit on the god's gift. The Purified are not enemies; they are **stillborn**.
- **Purity** - The obsession with "cleanliness" is the obsession with death. Nothing pure lives. Nothing pure breeds.
- **Fear** - To fear mutation is to fear the god itself. Cowards are not punished; they are **pitied**.
- **Sterility** - To refuse to breed (or to breed "pure" children) is to deny the future. The future is **filthy** or it is nothing.

**Taboos:**
- **Refusing the Spore** - When offered spores (in ritual or natural exposure), to reject them is apostasy.
- **Healing Without Mutation** - To heal a wound without letting it change you is to waste the lesson.
- **Killing Without Consuming** - If you kill something, eat it. Let its flesh become yours. Let the rot mediate the exchange.
- **Speaking of "Cures"** - There are no cures. There are only transformations you have not yet accepted.

## Hierarchy and Leadership

### Ranks and Titles (The Stages of Blooming)

The Rotborn are organized by **how much they have changed**. Each rank represents a stage of transformation—some physical, some spiritual, some both.

| Rank | Title | Transformation Required |
|------|-------|------------------------|
| 1 | **Seed** | Unchanged. New initiates. Still afraid. |
| 2 | **Sprout** | First voluntary mutation (spore-lung, worm-sibling, bone-shift). |
| 3 | **Bloom** | Multiple mutations. Visible to all. No longer passes as "normal." |
| 4 | **Gardener** - | Teaches others to welcome change. Performs mutation rituals. |
| 5 | **Flesh-Parent** | Has bred mutated children or "birthed" new members through ritual infection. |
| 6 | **The Twisted** | Council member. So changed that they are barely recognizable as human. |
| 7 | **The First Womb** | Supreme leader. A living incubator for the god's dreams. Pregnant with something that is not human. |

**Note:** Transformation is not always voluntary. Some members are "blessed" by accidental exposure, spontaneous mutation, or divine intervention. The Rotborn celebrate these as **chosen by the rot**.

### Leadership Council: The Circle of Flesh

The Rotborn are ruled by **The Circle of Flesh**—seven Twisted who have changed so profoundly that they communicate through shared hallucination. They meet in the **Womb-Chamber**, a living temple grown from calcified god-flesh.

**The Seven Twisted:**

- **The First Womb** - Supreme leader. Appears as a pregnant form (gender irrelevant). The pregnancy has lasted 17 years. What grows inside sings in the god's voice.
- **The Many-Mouthed** - Speaks for the council. Has 12 mouths distributed across their body. Each mouth speaks a different truth. When all 12 agree, it is the word of the god.
- **The Bone-Dancer** - Oversees mutation rituals. Their skeleton is external, articulated, moves independently of flesh. They "dance" by rearranging their bones.
- **The Spore-Mother** - Controls spore distribution. Her lungs are visible through translucent skin. Spores swirl inside her like galaxies. She breathes them out to bless initiates.
- **The Worm-King** - Commands the symbiotic fauna. His body is host to 1,000+ worm-siblings. They speak through him. He is never alone.
- **The Flesh-Weaver** - Creates living architecture. Can shape god-flesh like clay. The Festering Heart's temples are their sculptures.
- **The Dream-Eater** - Interprets the god's nightmares. Has no eyes; sees only in dreams. When awake, they are blind. When asleep, they see everything.

### Succession and Promotion

**Promotion** is not earned through service. It is **bestowed by the rot**:

- **Seed → Sprout:** Voluntary exposure ritual. The initiate inhales concentrated spores, drinks rot-water, allows worm-siblings to burrow into their flesh. If they survive without rejecting the change, they Sprout.
- **Sprout → Bloom:** The first visible mutation manifests. An extra eye. A second spine. Skin that weeps nectar. The community celebrates. The Bloom is no longer fully human—and that is the point.
- **Bloom → Gardener:** The member teaches another to change. Success is not required; the attempt is. The rot values **intent** over outcome.
- **Gardener → Flesh-Parent:** The member "births" a new Rotborn—either through biological reproduction (mutated child) or ritual infection (turning an outsider).
- **Flesh-Parent → Twisted:** The rot chooses. There is no ritual. One day, the member wakes changed—so changed that the Circle must accept them. To refuse a Twisted is to refuse the god.
- **Twisted → First Womb:** The current First Womb "births" their pregnancy. What emerges either kills them (unworthy) or becomes the new First Womb (worthy). The process is... messy.

## Territory and Architecture

### The Festering Heart (The Womb of the World)

The Rotborn control **the Festering Heart**—the region where the god's torso once was. This is the wettest, warmest, most **fertile** region of the world. The rot spreads fast here. The air is thick with spores. The ground pulses like a living thing.

**Geography:**
- **The Rib-Valleys** - Massive bone-arches (the god's ribs) creating sheltered valleys where life blooms in profusion
- **The Spore-Sea** - A literal ocean of spores, swirling in a basin where the god's heart once beat
- **The Womb-Temples** - Living structures grown from god-flesh, pulsing, breathing, birthing
- **The Mutation Pools** - Natural pools of rot-water where the faithful go to transform

### Architectural Principles

Rotborn architecture is not built. It is **grown**.

1. **Organic** - No straight lines. No angles. Everything curves, flows, breathes. Buildings are alive.
2. **Symbiotic** - Structures merge with inhabitants. Walls grow around residents. Floors digest waste. Ceilings exhale oxygen.
3. **Mutable** - Buildings change over time. Rooms grow. Corridors shift. A house today may be a cathedral tomorrow.

**Materials:**
- **God-Flesh** - Harvested (respectfully) from the decaying corpse. Still alive, still responsive.
- **Bone-Matter** - Calcified bone, shaped by the Flesh-Weaver into living forms.
- **Spore-Silk** - Woven by giant spore-spiders. Stronger than steel, breathable, biodegradable.
- **Worm-Cement** - Secreted by giant worms. Hardens into a substance like living concrete.

### Key Locations

#### The Womb-Chamber (Council Seat)
- **Purpose:** Where the Circle of Flesh meets. Also where initiates are "birthed" into the faction.
- **Features:**
  - The **Umbilicus** - A massive cord connecting the chamber to the god's remaining nervous system. The Circle listens through it.
  - The **Birth-Pools** - Where new members emerge from amniotic fluid, changed, reborn.
  - The **Hall of Faces** - Walls lined with the preserved faces of those who achieved full transformation. They watch. They approve.

#### The Spore-Sea (Sacred Site)
- **Purpose:** Pilgrimage site. The faithful come to breathe deep, to let the spores fill their lungs, to become one with the rot.
- **Features:**
  - The **Breathing Shore** - Where the spore-ocean laps at the coast. The tide is the god's respiration.
  - The **Drowning Altars** - Initiates submerge themselves, let spore-water fill their lungs. Some drown. Most emerge changed.
  - The **Vision-Reefs** - Coral-like spore formations that induce prophetic hallucinations.

#### The Flesh-Markets (Hub)
- **Purpose:** Trade, socialization, mutation services.
- **Features:**
  - **The Graft-Stalls** - Where body parts are traded. Want an extra arm? A second set of lungs? Eyes that see spores? Here.
  - **The Breeding-Pits** - Where the faithful go to conceive mutated children. Surrogates available.
  - **The Symbiosis-Bazaars** - Trade in worm-siblings, spore-sacs, living tools.

#### The Garden of Unasked Forms (Experimental Zone)
- **Purpose:** Where the Flesh-Weaver creates new life forms. Some are beautiful. Some are nightmares. All are **beloved**.
- **Features:**
  - **The Chimeric Groves** - Creatures with too many limbs, too many eyes, too many voices. They sing in harmony.
  - **The Womb-Trees** - Living trees that grow human fetuses in their branches. The fetuses are not human. They are **next**.
  - **The Flesh-Forge** - Where the Flesh-Weaver sculpts new forms from raw god-matter.

## Relationships with Other Factions

### Rotborn vs. The Purified

**Status: Holy War**

The Purified are not enemies. They are **heretics**. They cut away the god's gifts. They castrate themselves and call it purity. The Rotborn do not hate them. They **pity** them. And they will force the transformation upon them—out of love.

**The Horror:** The Rotborn do not kill Purified prisoners. They **infect** them. They hold them down and feed them spores. They watch as the transformation takes hold. They weep with joy as their enemy becomes family.

**Military Posture:**
- Eternal raids into Purified territory—not to conquer, but to **seed**
- Spore-bombers release clouds over Purified settlements. The Purified burn them. The Rotborn mourn the wasted gift.
- When a Purified settlement falls, the Rotborn do not occupy it. They **transform** it. Within a year, it is unrecognizable. Alive. Breathing.

### Rotborn vs. The Delusion Architects

**Status: Ideological Opposition**

The Architects are **cowards** who look into the abyss and lie about what they see. They curate delusions, engineer collective fictions, pretend that sanity is possible. The Rotborn know better: sanity is a lie. Sanity is **stasis**. And stasis is death.

**The Horror:** The Rotborn infiltrate Architect cities and release spores. Not to kill—to **liberate**. When an Architect citizen wakes with spore-lungs, the Rotborn celebrate. The Architects call it "contamination." The Rotborn call it **awakening**.

**Diplomatic Stance:**
- Trade is permitted (the Architects need mutation services, even if they deny it)
- Rotborn agents operate openly in Architect territory (they are not subtle; they do not need to be)
- When an Architect's delusion fails, the Rotborn are there to catch them: *"You see now. The rot was always truth. Come. Let us show you how to live with it."*

### Rotborn vs. The God's Nervous System

**Status: Religious Schism**

The God's Nervous System are **siblings**—but misguided ones. They implant spores and hear the god's dreams. So do the Rotborn. But the Prophets think the god is **speaking**. The Rotborn know better: the god is **dreaming**. And dreams are not commands.

**The Horror:** When Prophets and Rotborn meet, they do not fight. They **merge**. Rotborn open their flesh. Prophets offer their spores. Together, they become something new. Sometimes, the fusion works. Sometimes, it creates abominations. Both outcomes are celebrated.

**Diplomatic Stance:**
- Open borders. Prophets are always welcome in the Festering Heart.
- Joint rituals: Prophets provide the vision. Rotborn provide the flesh. Together, they birth something neither could alone.
- Disagreements are settled through **fusion trials**—both sides merge, experience each other's truth, emerge changed.

## Key NPCs

### The First Womb

**Title:** Supreme Leader of the Rotborn  
**Age:** Irrelevant (time is measured in pregnancies, not years)  
**Appearance:** A massively pregnant form, belly distended, skin translucent. Through the skin, something moves—too many limbs, too many eyes, a face that is not a face. The belly pulses with its own heartbeat. The First Womb's own body is secondary: a vessel for what grows inside. Breasts leak milk that glows with spore-light. Hips widened beyond human proportion. When they walk, the ground trembles.

**History:**
The First Womb was once called Elara. Seventeen years ago, they were a Seed—unchanged, afraid, begging for transformation. The Spore-Mother took them into the Womb-Chamber. They emerged... pregnant.

No one knows how. No one knows **what** impregnated them. The pregnancy has lasted seventeen years. The child—**the thing**—has grown so large that Elara can no longer walk without assistance. They are carried on a litter of living flesh, tended by Gardeners.

The thing inside speaks. Not in words. In **feelings**. In visions. In the language of the god's dreams. The First Womb translates. Their translations are never questioned.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To birth the thing inside. To hold it. To love it. To let it **lead**.
- **Secondary:** To transform every living thing on the god's corpse. No one should be denied the gift.
- **Hidden:** Sometimes, they fear what they carry. Sometimes, they wonder if the Purified were right. They suppress these thoughts. They are **weakness**.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Rotborn: The First Womb offers them a gift: a spore, a worm, a piece of the thing inside. Accept and become family. Refuse and become... lost.
- If player opposes the Rotborn: The First Womb does not order your death. They order your **transformation**. You will be captured. You will be seeded. You will emerge changed—and grateful.
- If player is neutral: The First Womb watches. They see potential in you. They send gifts: spore-sacs, worm-siblings, invitations to the Womb-Chamber.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The First Seed" - Player is offered a spore. Inhale it. Hallucinate. Emerge changed (minor mutation).
- **Major Chain:** "The Seventeen-Year Birth" - Player witnesses the pregnancy's progression. The thing inside grows. It speaks louder. The First Womb weakens. The birth approaches.
- **Endgame:** "The Womb Opens" - The birth happens. What emerges is... not knowable. The player must choose: worship it, kill it, or **become** it.

**Dialogue Style:**
Dreamy. Ecstatic. Speaks in metaphors of birth, growth, transformation. When the thing inside moves, their voice becomes multiple voices.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"You fear the change. I understand. I was afraid too. Seventeen years ago, I was... empty. Unchanged. A vessel with nothing to carry.*
>
> *Then It came. It chose me. It grew. And I understood: I was not a person. I was a **womb**.*
>
> *You can be a womb too. Not literally. Metaphorically. Let the rot fill you. Let it grow. Let it change you.*
>
> *It hurts. Oh, it hurts so beautifully. But pain is the price of becoming.*
>
> *Will you pay it?"*

### The Many-Mouthed

**Title:** Speaker for the Circle of Flesh  
**Age:** 34 (body); mouths range from 1 year to 10 years old  
**Appearance:** A human-form body with 12 mouths distributed across it: one on the face (original), two on the chest, three on the back, four on the limbs, two on the palms. Each mouth has its own voice, its own personality, its own truth. The mouths argue. Sometimes, they agree. When all 12 agree, it is the word of the god.

**History:**
The Many-Mouthed was once a Delusion Architect—a diplomat who negotiated with the Rotborn. During a negotiation, the Spore-Mother offered them a gift: a spore that would "let them speak all truths." They accepted.

The spore did not kill them. It **multiplied** them. The first new mouth appeared on their chest, screaming truths they had never spoken. Then another. Then another. Twelve mouths, twelve truths. The Architects exiled them. The Rotborn welcomed them.

Now, they speak for the Circle. Each mouth represents a different faction, a different philosophy, a different **lie**. When the mouths harmonize, the truth emerges.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To speak the final truth—the one truth that will unite all factions.
- **Secondary:** To grow more mouths. Thirteen. Fourteen. Until they are nothing but mouth, nothing but truth.
- **Hidden:** One mouth—the original—wants to be silent. It is tired. It wants to rest. It will never admit this.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Rotborn: The Many-Mouthed becomes their guide. Each mouth offers different advice. The player must learn which mouth to trust.
- If player opposes the Rotborn: The mouths argue about the player's fate. Some want conversion. Some want consumption. The player must play the mouths against each other.
- If player is neutral: The Many-Mouthed offers prophecies. Some are true. Some are lies. The player must discern which is which.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The Twelve Truths" - Each mouth gives the player a different task. Complete all twelve. The rewards contradict each other. Choose wisely.
- **Major Chain:** "The Thirteenth Mouth" - A new mouth begins to grow. It does not speak. It **sings**. The song is the god's lullaby. The player must help it emerge—or silence it forever.
- **Endgame:** "The Harmony" - All mouths must agree on a single truth. The player mediates. The truth they choose determines the game's ending.

**Dialogue Style:**
Polyphonic. Multiple voices speaking simultaneously. Sometimes in harmony. Sometimes in discord. Transcripts show all 12 voices.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *[Mouth 1 (face):] "You are lost."*
> *[Mouth 2 (chest-left):] "You are found."*
> *[Mouth 3 (chest-right):] "You are both."*
> *[Mouth 4 (back-top):] "Listen."*
> *[Mouth 5 (back-middle):] "Do not listen."*
> *[Mouth 6 (back-bottom):] "Listening is transformation."*
> *[Mouth 7 (left-arm):] "The rot is gift."*
> *[Mouth 8 (right-arm):] "The rot is burden."*
> *[Mouth 9 (left-palm):] "The rot is choice."*
> *[Mouth 10 (right-palm):] "Choice is illusion."*
> *[Mouth 11 (abdomen):] "We speak for the god."*
> *[Mouth 12 (groin):] "We are the god."*
>
> *[All mouths, in unison:] "What will you become?"*

### The Spore-Mother

**Title:** Keeper of the Spore-Sea  
**Age:** 62 (lungs are 3 years old; original lungs rejected, replaced)  
**Appearance:** A gaunt figure with translucent skin across the chest. Through the skin, her lungs are visible—vast, branching, glowing with spore-light. The spores swirl inside her like galaxies. When she breathes, the spores pulse. When she speaks, spores escape with each word. Her face is covered by a mask of living spore-silk, filtering nothing.

**History:**
The Spore-Mother was once a Prophet—a member of the God's Nervous System. She heard the god's dreams more clearly than any Prophet in her generation. But she wanted more. She wanted to **become** the dream.

She underwent the Ritual of Lung-Birth: her original lungs were excised. In their place, the Spore-Sea was implanted. Now, she breathes for the entire faction. Her exhalations bless initiates. Her inhalations purify the air.

She has not left the Festering Heart in 20 years. She does not need to. The spores carry her consciousness. Wherever spores exist, **she exists**.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To spread the spores to every corner of the god's corpse. Every breath should carry transformation.
- **Secondary:** To find someone who can breathe her spores without changing. She believes such a person exists. She will find them. She will **study** them.
- **Hidden:** She is dying. The spore-lungs are rejecting her. She has years, maybe months. She seeks a successor. She will not admit she is afraid.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Rotborn: The Spore-Mother offers them the Gift of Breath—a spore that merges with their lungs. They can now breathe underwater, in vacuum, in rot-zones. They are one of hers.
- If player opposes the Rotborn: The Spore-Mother hunts them. Not to kill—to **seed**. She releases personalized spores, engineered to bypass the player's defenses. She will transform them, even if they fight.
- If player is neutral: The Spore-Mother watches their respiration. She comments on their breathing patterns. She offers improvements. She is always right.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The First Breath" - Player inhales the Spore-Mother's breath. They hallucinate. They emerge with a minor lung mutation (breathe underwater, filter toxins, etc.).
- **Major Chain:** "The Spore-Kingdom" - The Spore-Mother's consciousness begins to fragment. Her spores develop independent wills. They form a kingdom inside her lungs. The player must navigate it, negotiate with the spore-kings, prevent civil war.
- **Endgame:** "The Last Exhalation" - The Spore-Mother dies. Her final exhalation releases all spores at once. The player must choose: let them spread (transform the world) or contain them (save the world from change).

**Dialogue Style:**
Breathy. Each word is accompanied by the sound of inhalation/exhalation. Spores escape with each syllable. When she is emotional, the spores change color.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"Breathe. [inhale] Feel the spores. [exhale] They are not your enemy. [inhale] They are your **future**. [exhale]*
>
> *Your lungs are so... small. So limited. I can fix that. I can make them like mine. Vast. Galactic. Full of **life**.*
>
> *It will hurt. Your lungs will tear. They will bleed. They will **become**.*
>
> *Say yes. Say yes and breathe with me. Say yes and **live**."*

### Seed Kael (Common Member Perspective)

**Title:** Seed (Initiate)  
**Age:** 19  
**Appearance:** Unchanged. Smooth skin. No visible mutations. No extra limbs, no glowing eyes, no worm-siblings. This is the point. Kael is what all Rotborn once were: **blank**. They wear simple robes of spore-silk, unadorned. They are beautiful in their potential.

**History:**
Kael was born in the Festering Heart, child of two Blooms. By all rights, they should have been mutated at birth. But they were not. They were... pure. The Rotborn watched them. Waited. Wondered if they were cursed.

At age 12, Kael asked for the Gift. The Spore-Mother herself oversaw the ritual. Kael inhaled. Kael drank. Kael opened their flesh to worm-siblings.

Nothing happened.

No mutation. No change. No transformation.

The Rotborn were confused. Some called Kael cursed. Some called them blessed. The First Womb intervened: *"They are not cursed. They are **waiting**. The rot will come when it is ready."*

Now, at 19, Kael is still a Seed. They are not ashamed. They are **patient**. They know the rot loves them. They know it will come. They wait. They breathe. They trust.

**What It Wants:**
- **Primary:** To transform. To become. To bloom. They have waited seven years. They are ready.
- **Secondary:** To understand why the rot has not touched them. Are they special? Are they broken? Are they... immune?
- **Hidden:** Sometimes, they fear the change. Sometimes, they wonder if the Purified were right. They suppress these thoughts. They are **weakness**. But they are there.

**Relationship to Player:**
- If player joins the Rotborn: Kael is their guide. They show the player the beauty of the Festering Heart. They do not mention their own lack of mutation. They hope the player's transformation will... inspire their own.
- If player opposes the Rotborn: Kael is sent to convert them. They believe if they can convince the player, the rot will finally recognize their faith. They try too hard. They desperation shows.
- If player is mutated: Kael is fascinated. They touch the player's mutations with reverence. They ask: "Does it hurt? Does it feel good? Do you hear the god's voice?" They want to know **everything**.

**Quests:**
- **Introduction:** "The Unchanged" - Kael asks the player to help them understand mutation. They visit mutation sites, talk to the transformed, witness rituals. The player sees the Rotborn through fresh eyes.
- **Major Chain:** "The First Sign" - Kael begins to change. A patch of skin glows. An eye shifts. A worm-sibling burrows in. Is it real? Or is it psychosomatic? The player must help them discern truth from hope.
- **Endgame:** "The Bloom" - Kael's transformation completes. What emerges is... unexpected. Not a standard mutation. Something new. Something the Rotborn have never seen. The player must help Kael accept what they have become.

**Dialogue Style:**
Earnest. Curious. Slightly desperate. They ask too many questions. They apologize for asking. They cannot stop asking.

**Sample Dialogue:**
> *"I know what you're thinking. 'This Seed, they're not really Rotborn. They're... normal.'*
>
> *I'm not normal. I'm **waiting**. There's a difference.*
>
> *The rot knows me. It knows I'm ready. It's just... taking its time. Like a good parent. Like a good **god**.*
>
> *You're mutated. I can see it. I can... touch? Would that be okay? I just want to feel what it's like. To know what I'm waiting for.*
>
> *Please?"*

## Quest Hooks and Story Arcs

### Introduction Quest Line: "The Womb Opens"

**Quest 1: "The First Seed"**
- **Trigger:** Player enters Rotborn territory
- **Objective:** Meet the Spore-Mother. Inhale her breath.
- **Choice:** Accept the spore (gain minor mutation, +Rotborn rep) or refuse (no change, -Rotborn rep but +Purified rep)
- **Reward:** Faction introduction, access to Rotborn quests (if accepted)

**Quest 2: "The Garden of Flesh"**
- **Trigger:** Completion of "The First Seed"
- **Objective:** Tour the Garden of Unasked Forms. Witness a mutation ritual.
- **Encounter:** Psychological horror. The ritual is graphic. The initiate screams. Then laughs. Then screams again.
- **Reward:** Understanding of Rotborn philosophy. Access to Flesh-Markets.

**Quest 3: "The Womb-Chamber"**
- **Trigger:** Completion of "The Garden of Flesh"
- **Objective:** Meet the First Womb. Receive their blessing.
- **Choice:** Accept the blessing (permanent mutation, locked into Rotborn path) or decline (remain neutral, keep options open)
- **Reward:** Rank of Sprout (if accepted), access to mutation services

### Major Faction Quest Chain: "The Seventeen-Year Birth"

**Overview:** The First Womb's pregnancy reaches its climax. The thing inside is ready to be born. The Rotborn prepare. The other factions panic. The player must choose: help the birth, stop it, or... become it.

**Birth 1: "The Quickening"**
- **Objective:** The thing inside moves for the first time. The First Womb convulses. The ground shakes. The player must stabilize them.
- **Gameplay:** Healing mini-game. Keep the First Womb alive while the thing inside thrashes.
- **Reward:** Trust of the First Womb. Access to inner Circle rituals.

**Birth 2: "The Contraction"**
- **Objective:** The First Womb's body begins to reject the pregnancy. Their flesh tears. The thing inside pushes. The player must... help it along. Or slow it down.
- **Choice:** Accelerate the birth (risk First Womb's life) or decelerate (risk the thing's wrath).
- **Reward:** Faction reputation. Mutation rewards (if accelerated).

**Birth 3: "The Dilation"**
- **Objective:** The Womb-Chamber must be prepared. The Circle of Flesh gathers. The Prophets are summoned. The player must coordinate.
- **Gameplay:** Diplomacy quest. Convince Purified observers to stay. Convince Architects to send healers. Convince Prophets to share their visions.
- **Reward:** Cross-faction alliances. Unique items from each faction.

**Birth 4: "The Rupture"**
- **Objective:** The water breaks. The thing inside screams. The First Womb begs for mercy. The player must... hold their hand. Or hold them down.
- **Gameplay:** Moral choice. Comfort the First Womb (humanity) or restrain them (efficiency). The thing inside notices.
- **Reward:** The thing's favor (if restrained). The First Womb's gratitude (if comforted).

**Birth 5: "The Crowning"**
- **Objective:** The thing emerges. It is... not describable. Too many limbs. Too many eyes. Too many voices. The First Womb dies. Or lives. Or becomes something else.
- **Choice:** Save the First Womb (kill the thing) or save the thing (let the First Womb die) or... merge them (become something new).
- **Reward:** Faction ending determination.

**Birth 6: "The First Cry"**
- **Objective:** The thing speaks its first word. It is the god's name. The player hears it. They change.
- **Gameplay:** Sanity challenge. The word is too much. The player must resist... or embrace.
- **Reward:** God-touched abilities. Or immunity. Or madness.

**Birth 7: "The Naming"**
- **Objective:** The thing must be named. The Circle argues. The mouths speak. The player decides.
- **Choice:** Name it after the First Womb (honor the dead) or name it after the god (honor the divine) or name it after yourself (claim it as your own).
- **Reward:** The thing's allegiance. Unique ending variation.

### Endgame Faction Resolution

**Scenario 1: The Birth Complete (Rotborn Victory)**
- **Condition:** Player helps the thing be born. The First Womb dies. The thing lives.
- **Outcome:** The thing becomes the new leader of the Rotborn. It is the god's avatar. The Rotborn begin a crusade to transform the entire world. The player is... part of it. Part of the thing. Part of the **we**.
- **Player Reward:** "God-Touched" title. Unique mutation abilities. Ending: The World Blooms.

**Scenario 2: The Womb Closes (Rotborn Defeat)**
- **Condition:** Player kills the thing. The First Womb lives. The Rotborn are shattered.
- **Outcome:** The First Womb goes mad. The Rotborn fracture. Some follow the player. Some follow the Many-Mouthed. Some follow nothing. The Festering Heart withers. The rot recedes.
- **Player Reward:** "Womb-Closer" title. Immunity to mutation. Ending: The Last Purity.

**Scenario 3: The Merger (Compromise)**
- **Condition:** Player merges with the thing. Becomes... both. Neither. Something new.
- **Outcome:** The player becomes the new First Womb. The thing inside them is the god's voice. The Rotborn do not conquer. They **invite**. The world transforms... willingly.
- **Player Reward:** "The Second Womb" title. Unique transformation abilities. Ending: The Gentle Bloom.

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors:**
- **Flesh-Pink** (#FFB6C1) - Living god-flesh, mutation, birth
- **Spore-Green** (#90EE90) - Glowing spores, life, transformation
- **Blood-Red** (#DC143C) - Vitality, sacrifice, the price of change
- **Bone-White** (#F8F8FF) - Not purity—potential. The canvas for mutation.

**Accent Colors:**
- **Worm-Gold** (#FFD700) - Symbiotic fauna, honored siblings
- **Amniotic-Blue** (#E0FFFF) - Birth-fluids, the womb, beginnings
- **Rot-Purple** (#800080) - Deep decay, the god's dreaming flesh

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Flesh-pink dominates architecture (living walls, breathing floors)
- Spore-green for lighting (bioluminescence, not electricity)
- Blood-red for rituals, sacrifices, important moments
- Bone-white only for initiates (they are blank canvases)

### Clothing and Mutation Styles

**Civilian "Clothing":**
- Living garments of spore-silk, grown not sewn
- Garments merge with skin over time. Removal is painful.
- No concealment. Mutations are displayed, not hidden.
- Colors indicate rank: pink (blooms), green (gardeners), red (flesh-parents)

**Ritual Vestments:**
- **Seeds:** Unadorned white robes (they have nothing to display)
- **Blooms:** Robes with openings for mutations (extra limbs, eyes, etc.)
- **Twisted:** No robes. Their bodies are the vestments.

**Mutation Aesthetics:**
- **Accepted:** Extra limbs, eyes, mouths, symbiotic organisms
- **Celebrated:** Visible organs, translucent skin, bioluminescence
- **Sacred:** Worm-siblings, spore-lungs, bone-externalization

### Symbols and Iconography

**Primary Symbol: The Open Womb**
- Stylized representation of a womb, birthing spores
- Represents transformation, birth, the god's creativity
- Used on banners, temples, ritual tools

**Secondary Symbols:**
- **The Spiraling Spore** - Spore with trailing spiral, represents growth
- **The Twisted Tree** - Tree with too many branches, represents mutation
- **The Worm-Ouroboros** - Worm eating its own tail, represents cycles

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Symbols are grown, not drawn (living tattoos, flesh-carvings)
- Always in motion (pulsing, breathing, shifting)
- Never symmetrical (symmetry is stagnation)

## Faction Mechanics (Gameplay Integration)

### Reputation System

**Reputation Scale:** -100 to +100

| Reputation | Title | Effects |
|------------|-------|---------|
| -100 to -76 | **Untouched** | Attacked by wild Rotborn, no faction interaction |
| -75 to -51 | **Resistant** | Offered "conversion" (forced mutation quests) |
| -50 to -25 | **Skeptical** | Allowed in outer territories, mutation services unavailable |
| -25 to +25 | **Seed** | Standard access, can begin mutation questline |
| +26 to +50 | **Sprout** | Minor mutations unlocked, discounts at Flesh-Markets |
| +51 to +75 | **Bloom** | Full mutation access, faction quests, breeding rights |
| +76 to +100 | **Twisted** | Honored member, unique abilities, council access |

**Reputation Changes:**
- **+5:** Accept mutation, complete faction quest
- **+10:** Complete major faction quest, witness birth ritual
- **+25:** Merge with Twisted member, become family
- **-5:** Refuse mutation, reject spore-gift
- **-10:** Harm Rotborn without cause
- **-50:** Kill Twisted member, desecrate Womb-Temple
- **+3:** Donate biomass, offer worm-siblings

### Faction Abilities

**Unlocked at Reputation +26 (Sprout):**
- **Spore-Breath** - Inhale toxins, exhale healing spores
- **Regenerative Flesh** - HP regen increased, scars become decorative
- **Symbiotic Sense** - Detect worm-siblings, communicate with fauna

**Unlocked at Reputation +51 (Bloom):**
- **Extra Limb** - Additional action in combat (AP +1)
- **Bioluminescence** - Illuminate dark areas, blind enemies
- **Flesh-Shaping** - Modify appearance at will, disguise as enemy

**Unlocked at Reputation +76 (Twisted):**
- **God-Touched** - Immunity to sanity damage, hear god's whispers
- **Metamorphosis** - Transform into combat form (stats shift, temporary)
- **Birth-Channel** - Summon worm-siblings, spore-clouds, living weapons

### Faction Vendors

**The Flesh-Markets:**
- Sells body parts, symbiotic organisms, mutation services
- Accepts biomass currency (organs, tissue, spore-sacs)
- Prices decrease with reputation

**The Graft-Stalls:**
- Sells temporary mutations (rental limbs, trial eyes)
- Run by Gardeners
- No reputation requirement (first mutation free)

**The Breeding-Pits:**
- Surrogate services, mutation inheritance planning
- Run by Flesh-Parents
- High reputation required (+50+)

## Notes for Implementation

- **Tone:** Ecstatic, not evil. The Rotborn are not villains. They are... enthusiastic. They genuinely believe they are helping.
- **Visuals:** Emphasize life, growth, fertility. Contrast with Purified sterility.
- **Music:** Organic, pulsing, rhythmic. Heartbeats, breathing, growth sounds.
- **Quests:** Always offer choice: accept transformation, resist transformation, redirect transformation.
- **Endgame:** The Rotborn should have a viable ending—not a "wrong" ending, but a costly one. Transformation is not free.
