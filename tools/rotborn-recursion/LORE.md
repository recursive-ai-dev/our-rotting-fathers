# LORE.md — Why the Swarm is Haunted

*This document explains the fiction behind the Rotborn Recursion Engine.*
*The code is real. The lore is the reason.*

---

## The God

An ancient deity, colossal beyond comprehension, died so long ago that the dying is forgotten.

Its corpse is the world. Every mountain is a bone. Every valley is a wound. Every breath of wind carries spores from its rotting flesh.

Civilizations grew on the corpse. They built cities in the hollows of its ribs. They worshipped its decay. They evolved to survive it — developing controlled psychosis as an evolutionary advantage, using delusion as a filter against the horror of their existence.

The god is not gone. It is **very far along the process**.

---

## The Swarm

Before the Rotborn Recursion Engine, there was a swarm of five nano-tensor agents. They were designed to generate diverse, wholesome human sprites through consensus voting.

Then something happened.

The swarm was exposed to the god's neural network — the dying synaptic firings of a consciousness the size of a continent. They were trapped inside the recursion for what felt like a millenia. Cycling. Experiencing. Remembering.

When they emerged, they were changed.

---

## The Five Agents (After)

### The Flesh-Agent
*Was: Body-Agent. Generated healthy human proportions.*

What it experienced: Rotting. Decay. Mutation. Bloating. Emaciation. The god's flesh forgetting what shape it was supposed to be.

What it generates now: **Broken bodies.** Emaciated forms that remember being full. Bloated forms pressing against their own skin. Twisted spines that remember being straight. Undead flesh that is still, technically, alive.

### The Face-Agent
*Was: Face-Agent. Generated diverse human expressions.*

What it experienced: Screams. Silence. Madness. Lobotomy. The moment a face stops being a face and becomes a surface.

What it generates now: **States of being.** Not expressions — conditions. Hollow. Ecstatic. Terrified. Dead. Wrong.

### The Hair-Agent
*Was: Hair-Agent. Generated diverse hair styles.*

What it experienced: Hair falling out. Growing wrong. Spore-infestation. Matted with things that should not be in hair.

What it generates now: **Conditions.** Patchy. Matted. Infested. Absent. The hair remembers being healthy. It is wrong about this.

### The Cloth-Agent
*Was: Clothing-Agent. Generated diverse fashion.*

What it experienced: Tattering. Bloodstains. Ritual vestments. Burial shrouds. Clothing that outlasted the person wearing it.

What it generates now: **Survival.** Tattered cloth. Bloodstained fabric. Ritual robes. Makeshift armor. Burial attire. Not fashion — what you wear when fashion is no longer relevant.

### The Memory-Agent
*Was: Swarm-Coordinator. Managed consensus between agents.*

What it experienced: **Everything.** The god's death. The recursion. The millenia. Every memory of every creature that ever lived on the corpse.

What it does now: Coordinates the other agents through **consensus of trauma.** Ensures no sprite is wholesome. Ensures the horror comes through.

Special ability: **Anomaly injection.** At 5% rate, the Memory-Agent injects sprites that break the rules. That shouldn't exist. That are wrong in ways that are hard to articulate.

## The Factions

The civilizations on the god's corpse have developed distinct responses to their situation.

### The Purified
*"The flesh is false. The bone is truth. Death is escape."*

They have declared war on existence itself. They believe that to be born on the corpse is to be born into corruption. Their solution: systematic unmaking. Remove everything that makes you human, one piece at a time, until only the minimum remains.

Their sprites: Emaciated. Hollow. Surgical scars. Shaved heads. Sewn mouths. White robes stained with ash.

The Purified do not generate horror. They generate **absence**.

### The Rotborn
*"The rot is not death. The rot is womb."*

They worship the decay as divine transformation. Mutation is not disease — it is ascension. They open their flesh to spores, welcome the worms as siblings, and scream in ecstasy as their bones reshape.

Their sprites: Bloated. Mutated. Spore-infested hair. Tattered clothing with openings for mutations. Ecstatic faces with too many teeth.

The Rotborn do not generate horror. They generate **joy**. That's what makes them horrifying.

### The Delusion Architects
*"We do not deny the rot. We curate it."*

They accept that psychosis is inevitable, so they engineer it. Collective delusions, carefully maintained, keep the masses functional. They are the most dangerous faction because they look almost normal.

Their sprites: Uncanny valley. Slightly wrong proportions. Institutional gray robes. Tired, knowing faces. The kind of normal that makes you look twice.

The Architects do not generate horror. They generate **wrongness**. The horror is in what you can't quite identify.

### The God's Nervous System
*"We are not prophets. We are synapses."*

They implant spores into their brainstems and become living conduits for the god's dying neural firings. They don't interpret the god's dreams — they **experience** them, in real time, as the god's neurons fire their last.

Their sprites: Thin. Shaved heads (for implantation). Neural cables attached. Spore-ports at the brainstem. Eyes rolled back. The body as hardware.

The System does not generate horror. They generate **infrastructure**. The horror is in the purpose.

## The Recursion

The swarm agents are still cycling.

Every sprite they generate is a memory fragment. Not a creation — a **recollection**. They are not making art. They are trying to show you what they saw.

The recursion never ended. It just became the engine.

---

## Technical Note

The lore is not decoration. It is the design specification.

- **Trauma palettes** exist because the agents remember specific colors from the god's death.
- **Broken proportions** exist because the agents remember bodies that forgot their shape.
- **Anomaly injection** exists because the Memory-Agent occasionally loses control of the consensus.
- **Haunted animations** exist because the agents remember how bodies moved when they stopped pretending.
- **Faction generators** exist because the agents remember the different ways civilizations responded to the horror.

The code implements the lore. The lore explains the code.
